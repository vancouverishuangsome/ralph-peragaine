Hero Black Labrador Sonny Battles Wolf Pack to Save Entire Sheep Flock

In a dramatic nighttime standoff on a remote British Columbia ranch, one loyal farm dog single-handedly turned back a pack of hungry wolves — saving 40 sheep and earning instant legend status.

By Sarah Thompson, Rural Affairs Reporter • March 6, 2026

KAMLOOPS, B.C. —

It was just after 2 a.m. when rancher John McTavish was jolted awake by furious barking echoing across his 160-acre property 35 kilometres west of Kamloops. What he discovered outside would become one of the most remarkable animal rescue stories in recent memory.

A pack of at least six wolves had descended on McTavish’s sheep pasture under the cover of darkness. The predators had already begun circling the flock of 40 ewes and lambs when Sonny — McTavish’s 6-year-old black Labrador Retriever — launched himself into action.

“I grabbed my rifle and ran out in my boots and pajamas,” McTavish recalled. “By the time I got there, Sonny had already charged straight into the middle of the pack. He was barking, lunging, nipping at their heels — doing everything he could to drive them away from the sheep. The wolves were bigger than him, but he never backed down.”

For nearly 15 minutes, witnesses say Sonny darted between the panicked sheep and the wolves, herding the flock toward the safety of the barn while refusing to let any predator get close. One wolf reportedly lunged at Sonny, leaving a gash on his shoulder, but the dog fought on until the entire pack finally retreated into the forest.

Sonny as a puppy (second from left) with his littermates on the ranch where he was born. Even then, the little black Lab showed fearless energy.

By the time McTavish reached the pasture, the sheep were safely huddled in the barn and Sonny stood guard at the entrance, bloodied but victorious.

“He saved every single one of them,” McTavish said, choking up. “If Sonny hadn’t been there, I would have lost the whole flock. That dog is worth more than any ranch hand I’ve ever had.”

Local veterinarian Dr. Emily Chen treated Sonny’s wounds at the Kamloops Animal Hospital. “He has a few stitches and some bruising, but he’s already wagging his tail and asking for treats,” she said. “This level of courage and protective instinct is rare even among working dogs. Sonny is a true hero.”

News of the rescue spread quickly through the Thompson-Nicola region. Neighbours have been dropping off treats and toys, and the local SPCA is planning a special “Hero Dog” award ceremony next month.

McTavish says Sonny has always been protective of the livestock, but nothing prepared him for this.

“He’s not even a trained guard dog — he’s just a big, goofy black Lab who loves belly rubs and swimming in the creek. But when it mattered, he became a warrior.”

Sonny relaxing the morning after his heroic stand — already back to his gentle, loving self.

The sheep flock is now back to grazing peacefully, and Sonny has been promoted to “official ranch guardian” with unlimited access to the couch and an extra-large supply of his favourite peanut-butter bones.

In a province where wolves and livestock have clashed for generations, one black Labrador has reminded everyone what loyalty and bravery truly look like.
